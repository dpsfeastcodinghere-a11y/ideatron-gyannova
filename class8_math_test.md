## Questions:

### Rational Numbers
1.  Define rational numbers and give two examples.
2.  Represent -3/4 on a number line.
3.  Find five rational numbers between 1/2 and 3/4.
4.  **MCQ:** Which of the following is a rational number?
    a) √2
    b) π
    c) 0.333...
    d) All of the above
5.  **MCQ:** The additive inverse of -7/19 is:
    a) 7/19
    b) -7/19
    c) 19/7
    d) -19/7

### Linear Equations in One Variable
6.  Solve for x: 2x - 3 = 7.
7.  The sum of two numbers is 95. If one exceeds the other by 15, find the numbers.
8.  **MCQ:** Which of the following is a linear equation in one variable?
    a) x² + 2x + 1 = 0
    b) 3x + 5 = 11
    c) x + y = 7
    d) 1/x + 2 = 5
9.  **MCQ:** If 5x - 7 = 2x + 8, then x is equal to:
    a) 5
    b) 3
    c) 15
    d) 1

### Understanding Quadrilaterals
10. Define a quadrilateral and list its types.
11. The angles of a quadrilateral are in the ratio 3:5:9:13. Find all the angles of the quadrilateral.
12. **MCQ:** A quadrilateral whose all sides are equal and all angles are 90 degrees is called a:
    a) Rhombus
    b) Rectangle
    c) Square
    d) Parallelogram
13. **MCQ:** The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is:
    a) 180°
    b) 360°
    c) 90°
    d) 270°

16. **MCQ:** To construct a unique quadrilateral, how many measurements are generally required?
